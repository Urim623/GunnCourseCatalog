World Language

5 Languages: French, German, Japanese, Mandarin Chinese, and Spanish

Each Language Programs usually has 4 levels: 1, 2, 3, and AP., but some languages have electives.

Students who successfully began their language studies in middle school move directly into level 2. Students coming from out of the PAUSD district and/or who are native or heritage speakers or have been in after-school or immersion programs are required to take a placement exam to determine their appropriate placement.

AP World Language classes are advanced courses for students aiming for college-level proficiency. They focus on fluency in speaking, reading, writing, and listening while analyzing cultural themes. Students prepare for the AP exam to demonstrate their mastery.

  • Prior Recommended Courses: Level 3 or department approval; grades 10-12.
  • Homework Expectation: Up to 2 hours per week.

Additional French Courses

French Civilization & Culture

This advanced French course helps students achieve proficiency in the Intermediate High to Advanced-Low range, focusing on communication skills. Students analyze historical, political, and social issues of French-speaking cultures through literature, films, music, art, and media. They enhance their language skills through presentations, research, and reflections, with opportunities to explore topics of personal interest in-depth.

  • Prior Recommended Course(s): French AP Language or department approval; grades 11-12.
  • Homework Expectation: Up to 2 hours per week.

Additional Spanish Courses

Spanish/Spanish 1

Spanish/Spanish 2

Spanish for Spanish Speakers (S/S) classes are for native Spanish speakers to improve their communication skills and cultural understanding. S/S 1 focuses on basic communication tasks and topics like family, school, and travel, while S/S 2 advances to more complex tasks and themes such as technology, art, and global challenges. Both levels help students connect language use with culture and other academic areas.

  • Prior Recommended Courses: Span/Span 1: ability to speak and understand Spanish due to heritage or immersion education, but little or no reading and writing ability; Span/Span 2: ability to speak and understand Spanish due to heritage or immersion education, with some reading and writing ability, S/S 1 or department approval; grades 9-12.
  • Homework Expectation: Up to 1 hours per week.

Spanish Civilization & Culture H

This honors Spanish course helps students achieve proficiency in the Intermediate High to Advanced-Low range, focusing on communication skills. Students explore historical, political, and social issues of Spanish-speaking cultures through literature, films, music, art, and media. They develop language skills through presentations, research projects, and reflections, with opportunities to delve deeper into topics of personal interest.

  • Prior Recommended Courses: Spanish 3 or department approval; grades 9-12.
  • Homework Expectation: Up to 1 hours per week.
